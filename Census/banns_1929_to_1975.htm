The Register of Banns of Marriage from 1929 to 1975 is a partial substitute
for the missing Marriage Register covering this period. It shows the prospective
husbands and wives, usually their status, and the Parish where they resided.
Usually, the couple would marry in the wife's Parish Church, but not always.

The Banns are customarily read for three successive weeks, and this list shows
the date of the first Banns, plus any comment written in the Register. Note how
the number of marriages increased sharply from 1939 onwards as the steady stream
of soldiers passed through the nearby Garrison.

There were some other weddings at Arborfield during this time, but they were by
Licence obtained from the Archbishop of Canterbury.

Scroll along to the right to see all of the columns. Note that the abbreviation
'E.R.T.P.' stands for 'Electoral Roll of This Parish'.
