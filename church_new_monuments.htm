MONUMENTAL INSCRIPTIONS

ARBORFIELD BERKSHIRE NEW CHURCH


Copy of the transcript by L. H. Chambers, 5 Blandford Road, St Albans Herts.

visited June 15th 1929

held in Reading Reference Library


Full list of Inscriptions in the new Churchyard.

Commencing at southwest part of churchyard west of the path leading from entrance gate to the path tracking west to north.

[NOTE: Much of the list is in abbreviations, but most have been spelt-out here in full where the meaning is known. Two frequently-occurring abbreviations are 'w.f.e.' (Words facing east) and 'w.f.w.' (Words facing west).]

1 On a grey granite cross of 2 tiers, w.f.e.

Charles Swinton Williamson, died August 2, 1928, aged 48. Goodbye sweetheart, till the day dawn.
2 1½ yd south from 1 on a white marble kerb memorial

Douglas Wilfred Churcher; March 21st 1865, November 8th, 1925; (late) Lt. Colonel Royal Irish Fusiliers; ‘Love is strong in death’.
3 2½ yds west from 2 on a dark grey polished marble cross with base containing the inscription:

In ever loving memory of Hubert May of "Whitley Grove" Reading, Born May 7th 1860, Died November 29th 1924.
4 1½ yd north from 3 on a chevron stone slab of 3 tiers with full length carved cross on surface.

In loving memory of William Cordery Junior, who was called to rest April 21st 1891 aged 42 years. Lord Jesus receive my spirit.

In affectionate remembrance of Sarah the beloved wife of William Cordery, Junior, who departed this life March 1876 aged 31 years. Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord.
5 3 yds from 4 on a t u massive chest shaped tomb enclosed with tall iron rails.

North centre: Washington Jackson Esquire, youngest son of James Jackson Esquire, of County Monaghan by Mary Daughter of Norman Steele Esquire of Monalty Carrickmacross, Born January 21st 1784 Died at his residence 41 Belgrave Square London July 14th 1865.

North left: Anna Maria Widow of Washington Jackson Esquire and eldest Daughter of the late George Dawson, Ferriby, Yorkshire, Captain of Colonel Tarleton’s Regiment of Light Dragoons, Born at Burlington New Jersey October 22nd 1798 Died at 41 Belgrave Square London October 7th 1879 "Her children arise up and call her blessed".

North right: Anna Maria Widow of Franklin W. McCoy Esquire of Mobile Alabama, U.S.A., eldest Daughter of the late Washington Jackson Esquire, Born at Philadelphia U.S.A. July 13th 1821 Died September 26th 1894 at her residence 93 Leaham Gardens London.

South centre: In loving memory of W. Washington Jackson, Born at Philadelphia U.S.A. 1824 Died at Town Thorns Rugby 1898 Interred at Brinklow. Also of Norman Washington Jackson, Born at Philadelphia, 1831 Died at Mattapoisett, Mass., U.S.A., 1906, Interred at Salem, Mass., U.S.A.

South left: In loving memory of James Washington Jackson, Born at Philadelphia, U.S.A., 1830, Died at 61 Onslow Square London 1896, Interred in Brompton Cemetery. Also of Mary, widow of George Martin Esquire, and daughter of Washington Jackson, Born at Philadelphia 1822 Died at 2 Cromwell Gardens 1893, Interred in Brompton Cemetery.

South right: In loving memory of George Dawson youngest son of the late Washington Jackson Esquire, Died October 16th 1914 aged 73.

East: In loving memory of Ellen Thirkman youngest Daughter of Washington Jackson, She fell asleep 19th December 1900. I will lay me down in peace and sleep for thou Lord only makest me dwell in safety.

North [illegible]: The Family Vault of Washington Jackson Esquire.

West: In loving memory of Harriet third Daughter of Washington Jackson, Died 13 May 1900. Born 1826 Died 1900.
6 5 yd from 5 on a very tall white marble cross with box containing inscription and a flat round stone on the grave also with an inscription.

In memory of Thomas Hargreaves of Arborfield Hall and the Mount Bishopstoke Esquire, J.P. and Hon. Lieut. R.N.R. eldest son of John Hargreaves of Broad Oak Lancashire, Born September 21 1832, Died September 28 1891, and of Sarah, wife of the above, Daughter of Washington Jackson, Born December 6 1833, Died February 23 1918.

Grave stone: God is love. In memory of Arthur, eldest son of Thomas Hargreaves Esquire, of Arborfield Hall, Born August 20th 1859, Died October 25th 1878. Just as I am without one plea, But that thy blood was shed for me; And that Thou bidst me come to Thee, O Lamb of God I come.
7 4 yds north from 6 on a chevron stone slab.

In memory of Henry Chandler who lived in the family of Thomas Hargreaves Esquire for 58 years. Aged 78 years.

Maria Chandler Died 30th April 1923 Aged 82 years. Peace perfect peace.
8 2 ft north from 7 on another chevron stone slab.

In loving memory of Ellen Jane Greenwood who entered into rest March 9th 1903 aged 48 years. For ever with the Lord.
9 1 yd east from 8 on a chevron stone slab.

In memory of David Watts who lived in the family of Thomas Hargreaves forty four years aged 74 years. Watch and pray.

Also in memory of Elizabeth Watts, Born April 1822 Died 8 June 1907.
10 2½ ft east from 9 on another chevron stone slab.

In memory of Matilda wife of George Brown for many years nurse in the family of Thomas Hargreaves Esq. Born Sept 1830 Died May 1883.
11 3 yds north from 10 on a t.u. white marble cross with base containing inscription w.f.e.; this and 12 & 13 are inlaid with white marble kerb.

In memory of John Beauchamp Hayes (late Captain 12th Royal Lancers) and only son of The Revd. Sir John Warren Hayes, Bart. Born August 1st 1849 Died November 18th 1884. "And there shall be no more curse, but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him". Rev. XX, v.3. Also in memory of Julia Annie wife of the above, who died 8th May 1906.
12 On a very t. white marble cross of 3 tiers; w.f.w. a [illegible] cross.

Sacred to the memory of The Rev. Sir John Warren Hayes, Bt. Rector of this parish from 1839 to 1879. Born August 12th 1799 Died January 23rd 1896 also to Ellen his wife who died August 16th 1849. And now abideth faith, hope, charity, these three, but the greatest of these is charity.
13 On a short white marble cross of 3 tiers; w.f.e.

Sacred to the memory of Ellen Anne, Wife of John Simonds of Newlands, Born 18th August 1846, Died 9th December 1896. We know that we have passed from death unto life because we love the brethren.
14 2 yd. North from 13 on a light grey granite cross of 3 tiers; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Charles de Clare Richards, Born April 19th 1860 Died Feb 7th 1916, second son of the late Brigadier General W. N. Richards. "In thy light we shall see light".
15 1 yd north west from 14, on a t. col. Stone cross of 3 tiers.

In loving memory of Sarah Tayler, who died Feb. 7th 1883 aged 62 years, also of Charles husband of Sarah Tayler, who died Sept 21st 1884 aged 62 years.
16 1½ yd north from 15 on a light coloured stone of a [illegible] nature; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Robert Lambourne, who departed this life April 22, 1880 aged 63 years. "Thy will be done". Also of Sarah Ann his beloved daughter, who departed this life Jan 18, 1889 aged 28 years. Be ye also ready.

17 3 yds north east from 16 on a very tall stone:

In affectionate remembrance of Thomas Blackwell, who departed this life April 22, 1881, aged 73 years. In sure and certain hope of the resurrection to eternal life. Also of Ann wife of the above who died Dec 8th 1888, aged 84 years. Her end was peace.

18 2 yds north from 17 on a chevron stone slab.

In loving memory of James Downham, who departed this life April 3, 1880, aged 41 years. Be thou faithful unto death and I will give thee a crown of life.
19 2 ft north from 18 on a light stone cross of a [illegible] nature, w.f.e.

In affectionate remembrance of William James the beloved Son of James & Maria Downham, who departed March 3 1879 Aged 12 Years "The Lord gave and the Lord hath taken away".

20 At the foot of 19 on a t.u.

In loving memory of Nicholas John Mott, who died at Arborfield December 28th 1879 aged 41 years. As for man his days are as grass, as a flower of the field, so he flourisheth, Psalm 103 v 15.
21 1 yd north from 20.

Sacred to the memory of Sarah Catherine Clifford of Maidstone Kent who died at Arborfield on the 25th of November 1875 aged 70 years, she had been a most faithful and respected nurse in the families of James N. Daniels Esquire of Esher, Surrey, and the Revd. Sir John Hayes, Bt., Rector of this parish, for more than 40 years. Be ye also ready for in such an hour as ye think not, the son of man cometh. St. Matthew 24c 44v.
22 1½ yd south east from 20 on a s.u. cross; w.f.w.

In memory of Reginald Percy, son of Percy and Ada Lee of Carters Hill, Born January 27th 1880, Died May 22nd 1880. He shall gather the lambs with his arms and carry them in his bosom.
23 3 yds s.e. from 22 on a mound headed in white marble s.; w.f.e.

In loving memory of James Gould, Born September 17th 1817 Died February 21st 1903 – never forgotten – Also of Mary Gould Died Oct 31st 1913, aged 94 years.
24 2 yds south from 23 in a t.u. white marble s.; w.f.e.

In ever loving memory of William Vincent who was called to rest July 15th 1904. Aged 66 years. "Whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God". Also of Sarah Jane, wife of the above who fell asleep Dec. 7th 1916, aged 75 years. "His children arise up, and call him blessed".
25 3 yds north west from 24 on a s.u. white marble iron with rock base containing inscription; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Charles Reginald Bowyer, who died Feb 2nd 1902, aged 6 weeks. Also of Howard William Bowyer, who died Oct 27th 1906 aged 2 years 9 months, the dearly loved children of Ernest and Annie Bowyer. He took them up in his arms, AM 333.
26 1½ yd north west from 25 on a m.u. stone cross of a perishable nature; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Lucy Jane, wife of John Roe Gould of Carter’s Hill, Arborfield, who entered into rest September 13th 1884, aged 33. She is not dead but sleepeth.
27 1 yd north from 26 on a t.u. stone; w.f.e.

In affectionate remembrance of Elizabeth Walker, who died May 8th 1882 aged 57 years. "Her end was peace".
28 1½ yd north east from 27 on a t.u. polished red granite slab enclosed with short iron rails.

To the dear memory of George Piercy Savage, died December 11th 1902 aged 62 years. "Peace perfect peace". Also of Selina Savage his widow, died March 3rd 1907 aged 67 years. It is not night if thou be near.

In fond memory of Miriam the beloved wife of George Piercy Savage, Died December 3 1876 aged 35 years. "Not my will, but thine O Lord be done". Also Alice, daughter of the above, died July 7th 1886 aged 17 years.

29 2 yds north from 28 on a long raised stone slab enclosed with short iron rails; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Bessie Carter, who died August 16th 1872 aged 19 years. I have loved thee with an everlasting love therefore with loving kindness have drawn thee.
30 3½ yds north east from 29 on a t.u. white marble slab; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Samuel Cook, who died January 31st 1883 aged 60 years, also Elisabeth wife of the above, who died January 22nd 1908 aged 80 years. "Peace perfect peace".
31 5 yds from 30 on a large flat stone slab; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Rudolph Ernest Morelli Meyer, born October 1843, Died 21 October 1887.
32 1 yd from 31 on a cross head s.; w.f.e.

In affectionate remembrance of Richard Wells, who died 11th June 1889 in his 85th year. "And the world passeth away and the lust thereof but he that doeth the will of God abideth for ever". Also of Catherine Wells the beloved wife of the above Richard Wells, who departed this life April 27th 1893 aged 79 years. Looking unto Jesus.
33 1¼ yd south from 32 on a t.u. white marble slab; w.f.e.

In memory of Elizabeth wife of Henry W. Lednor, who died Dec 9th 1890 aged 73 years, also of Henry William Lednor, who died June 8th 1907 aged 82 years. His end was peace.
34 1 yd south from 33 on a s.u. white marble slab; w.f.e.

In memory of Lizzie the beloved wife of Arthur Charles Tayler, died Nov 4th 1926.
35 3 yds east from 34 on a t.u. grey granite cross; w.f.e.

Ralph Pomeroy Simonds, born May 15th 1884 Died Jan 20th 1916 (The result of a motor accident) second and dearly loved son of William and Beatrix Simonds of Farley Hill, Berks. "Greater love hath no man than this that a man lay down his life for his friends."

Lewis Lane Goddard, born August 1 1838, died April 29 1910. Naomi Goddard, Died February 17 1914 (aged 75 years).

38 1 yd north from 37 on a t.u. white marble slab; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Frederick Matthews the beloved husband of Elizabeth Matthews who died Feb 7th 1900 aged 64. Thy will be done". Also of Elizabeth Matthews, wife of the above, who died June 17th 1903, aged 64 years.
39 5½ yds north from 38 on a t.u. s; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Sarah, wife of David Brill for 33 years in the family of T. Hargreaves Esquire, Born 1836 Died 1889. God is love. David Brill Died April 10th 1909 aged 73.
40 4 yds east from 39 on a t.u.s.; w.f.w.

Sacred to the memory of Ann Saunders, Died September 14, 1889 aged 83 "Looking unto Jesus"; also of Stephen, son of the above, died December 5, 1884, aged 50.
41 7 yds south from 40 on a t.u. white marble s.; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Henry David, who died May 19th 1897, aged 77 years; also of Lucy (wife of the above) who died February 8th 1911 aged 85 years "For ever with the Lord".
42 1 yd south from 41 on a t.u. white marble s.; w.f.e.

I.H.S. Sacred to the memory of Frederick Barker who was called home December 11th 1902, aged 57 years. "There remaineth therefore a rest for the people of God", also of Anne wife of the above Died November 12th 1928 aged 82 years "At rest".

In loving memory of our dear Parents Thomas Haynes, who departed this life 4th July 1908 aged 69 years, and of Emma Haynes, who departed this life 4th June 1910 aged 72 years. Resting till the dawn breaks and shadows flee away.

In loving memory of Edith Bushell died March 16 1921 aged 59 years.

In loving memory of Lillian Ivy Bushell died Nov 21st 1923, aged 23 years. Also Lillian May, daughter of the above Died April 18th 1924, aged 5 months "At rest".

In loving memory of Mabel Garey daughter of Harriet New and the late James Garey, who fell asleep May 7th 1925, Thine for ever, God of Love.

In loving memory of Charles New, born January 29th 1859, accidentally killed March 11th 1921. "Thy way not mine O Lord".
48 1 ft north from 47 on a t.u. white marble s; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Alice Gertrude Garey, who fell asleep 15th March 1918 aged 35 years, daughter of Harriet New and of the late James Garey "The finger of God touched her and she slept".

In loving memory of Hannah, wife of Samuel Bentley, Born October 4th 1833 Died January 23rd 1912. Until the day break and the shadows flee away.

Also in loving memory of Samuel Bentley, Born June 23rd 1836, Died March 6th 1914 "They are in peace", Wisdom iii.3.

In loving memory of Emily Ann Barnes, Died August 12th 1921 aged 51 years; also of Edward Arthur, beloved son of the above, killed at Gonnehem April 23rd 1918 aged 21 years. They rest in peace.

In loving memory of Edward Maud Barnes (husband of the above), Died Dec 14th 1927 aged 58. Father in thy gracious keeping, leave we now thy servants sleeping.
52 5 yds north from 51 on a t.u. pointed south; w.f.w.

I.H.S. In affectionate remembrance of William Emblen who died Feb 13th 1889 aged 78 years. With Christ which is far better.
53 8 yds south east from 52 on a t.u. white marble cross of 3 tiers; w.f.e.

In loving memory of Kenneth Loder Cromwell Prescott, born September 13th 1875, died December 3rd 1922. The souls of the righteous are in the hand of God … and they rest in peace.
54 Close to 53, south from it on a small u. white marble cross of 3 tiers; w.f.e; this and no. 53 are enclosed with a white marble kerb.

In loving memory of Richard Cromwell Prescott, born 11th November 1919, Died 14th September 1920. "Jesus called a little child unto Him", Matt. xviii .2.

"In thy light shall we have light". In loving memory of James Hamilton Bruce, died 10th June 1906, aged 92 years, and of his wife Jessie Anne Ker Wilson, died 8th April 1927, aged 83 years. "I thank my God upon every remembrance of you", Phill 1.9.

The right honourable Thomas Ryburn Buchanan, Fellow of All Souls College, Oxford, a privy councillor twenty nine years member of parliament. Unto Thee O God do we give thanks yea unto Thee do we give thanks.

W. side of border: Let thine eyes look right on. In the way of righteousness there is life and in the pathway thereof there is no death.

In m.d.m. of Thomas Smiles, who died May 15th 1876 aged 31 years, also Ellen, daughter of the above, who died May 7th 1875 aged 2 years & 6 months.
98 2½ yds south from 97 on a massive chest shaped s.; inlaid with iron rails about 2½ ft high.

In memory of Eliza, wife of John Giles, who died March 21st 1865 aged 49 years. ‘Father thy will be done’.
99 2 ft north from 98 on a s.u.s.; w.f.w.

In loving memory of Eliza, wife of George Lowe, died May 12th 1899 aged 6. Now the labourer’s task is o’er.
100 1 ft north from 99 on a s.u.s.; w.f.w

In loving memory of George, eldest son of John & Marie Lowe who died June 15 1875 aged 44 years.
101 1½ yd north west from 100; on a s.w.s.; w.f.w.

In loving memory of Maria wife of John Lowe, who died Oct 31 1875, aged 72 years.
102 1½ yd north east from 101; on a s.u.s.; w.f.w.

In loving memory of John Lowe, who died Sep. 25 1878, aged 88 years.
103 1 yd north from 102 on a m.u.s.; w.f.w.

In memory of Ann Clacey, who departed this life September 26 1881 aged 58 years.
104 2 yds north from 103, on a m.u. stone cross of 3 tiers; w.f.e. with a very wide stone border.

In memory of Mary May, died Nov 22 1906. For 34 years a faithful servant in the family of John Simonds of Newlands. ‘By love serve one another’, Gal. 6.13.
105 8 yds east from 104, on a m.u.s.; w.f.e.

In affectionate remembrance of Joseph Allum the beloved husband of Jane Allum, who departed this life August 1st 1875 aged 77 years. There remaineth a rest to the people of God.
106 2 ft south from 105 on a m.u.s.; w.f.w.

In memory of Elizabeth only beloved daughter of Joseph & Jane Allum, who died Sep 7thth 1869 aged 29 years. Come unto me all ye that labour and are heavy laden and I will give you rest. I believe thy promise, O Lord of God I come.
107 1 yd south from 106 on a terra cotta cross head s’ w.f.e.

S.t.m.o. Kate the beloved child of James and Jane Emblen, who departed this life June 19 1864 aged 5 years.

108 1 yd south from 107 on a m.u.s.; w.f.w.

In affectionate remembrance of Charlotte, the beloved daughter of Charles and Ann Clacey, who died May 17th 1870 aged 8 years.
109 1½ yd south west from 118 on a chevron stone slab with full length carved cross on surface.

In remembrance of William J. Hannington, who died June 19 1886 aged 61 years. "Thy will, not mine, be done". In affectionate remembrance of Joel Over, who died April 17th 1872 aged 81 years, also Kezia, wife of the above, who died October 2 1873 aged 74 years. Many are the afflictions of the righteous but the Lord delivereth us out of them all. Psalm xxxiv.19.
110 1 yd east from 109, on a long coffin shaped stone slab with stone supports & enclosed with iron links which are now broken down on the south, east and north sides. A full length carved cross on surface of the slab.

Here lies Edward Ralph Charles Sheldon youngest son of E. R. C. Sheldon of Brailes Warwickshire, died November 26th 1867 aged 38.
111 3 yds south from 110, on a s.u.s.; w.f.e.

In memory of Annie Maclean daughter of Eliza Blackwell died Nov 29th 1869 aged 7 years. "My hour of labour past, I may trust in heaven at last, Thou from sin can’st set me free, Lord of life I trust in thee".
112 2½ yds north from 111 on a m.u.s.; w.f.w.

In memory of William Painton, died July 13th 1869 aged 48 years. What doth the Lord require of the but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God.
113 2½ yds west from 112 on a t.u.s; w.f.w.

Sacred to the memory of John Maynard, who died March 14th 1866, aged 81 years. "The Lord gave and the Lord hath taken away, blessed be the name of the Lord".
114 10 yds north west from 113 on a small wooden cross that is all perished but there is a glass case with a card inside which has on it:

Ann Franklin, who died March 6 88 aged 39 years.
115 2 yds south from 114 on a m.u.s.; w.f.w.

In affectionate remembrance of George Franklin aged 22 years, who met with a fatal accident whilst descending Sulham Hill near Reading Sep 4th 1869. This stone was erected by the butchers of Reading as a token of their respect and appreciation of him as a fellow worker. In the midst of life we are in death.
116 5 yds south east from 115 on a m.u.s.; w.f.w.

In memory of Anthony Cole, who died June 23rd 1866, aged 83 years. "Be ye also ready", also of Ann, his wife who died Oct 30th 1867, aged 80 years.
